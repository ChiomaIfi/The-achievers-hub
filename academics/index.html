Instead of memorising steps, try linking ideas together. Ask yourself why each process happens and how it leads to the next one. When you can explain the connection between steps, youre understanding the topic, not just remembering it.

Example: When you exercise, your muscles need more oxygen. Your heart rate increases to pump blood faster, and your breathing speeds up to take in more oxygen.

These changes are linked, one causes the next.

The Feynman technique is a good way to check whether you really understand a concept!
Memorising gives you surface-level knowledge, while understanding gives you in-depth knowledge, making it easier to link topics together.
Understanding concepts instead of just memorising them can help you remember things for longer. Once you understand something, its hard to forget it.
A lot of students think understanding and memorising are the same thing. Understanding means knowing why something works and being able to explain or apply it, while memorising is just repeating information without really knowing how or when to use it.

My go-to!
Mind maps:Mind maps break down complicated topics into simple connected points, making revision less daunting.
Feynman Technique:Explain a topic like youre teaching a five-year-old. Its a fun way to check if you really understand the content.
Flashcards:Great for revising topics and refreshing your memory. They work best after youve already learned a topic.
Pomodoro Technique: Study in short intervals with breaks in between. Its especially helpful if you find it hard to concentrate for long periods of time.
Active note-taking:Taking short notes during class or in your own time is a great way to remember content easily.
